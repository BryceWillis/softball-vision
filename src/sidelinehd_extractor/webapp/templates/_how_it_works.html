How this works

This app watches a SidelineHD game video, reads the scoreboard in the corner, and builds a list of timestamps — one per inning and one per at-bat — that you can paste into the video's YouTube description. Viewers can then jump straight to any at-bat.

  1. Add your team's roster. Jersey numbers and names, so the timestamps can say who was at bat. Do this before running a game — names are matched while the game is being read.
  2. Paste a game link. A YouTube link to your SidelineHD game video (or a playlist of games) and press Start.
  3. Wait while it reads the game. A full game takes roughly 30–45 minutes. You can leave this page open and watch the progress.
  4. Copy your timestamps. When it finishes, open the game page, double-check anything it flags, and copy the finished text into your YouTube description. You can also send (anonymized) feedback to help improve the app.

Everything runs on this computer. Player names stay in local files and are never uploaded; the feedback report replaces names with placeholders before anything leaves this machine.
